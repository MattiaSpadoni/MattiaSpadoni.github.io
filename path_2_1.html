The relic

Silvano deo / bono sacr(um). / Familia / Abundantior(um) / v(otum) l(ibens) solvit.

"Holy to the good God Silvano. Abundatiorum family observe willingly the promise."

It is difficult to read the inscription, but the latin tells us about a gift from the family of Abundanti (we are not certain about the provenance of this name) to a god of nature Silvano.

Don't be misguided, the gift was not the Epigraph. Look on the stone and you will see a little space. There was the gift, probably a little statue.

In Roman society was a common practice to dedicate gifts and epigraphs to Gods in order to thank them or to ask them favors and benevolence. This Epigraph is not an exception.

The God Silvano was a God of nature worshipped by Roman, its faith grew during Imperial age and this Epigraph was created at the beginning of III century AD
